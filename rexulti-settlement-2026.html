Rexulti Prescription Drug Settlement — $4.75 Million

Administered by: Consumer Law Group · Approved by: Superior Court of Quebec

Canadians who were prescribed and took the antipsychotic medication Rexulti between February 16, 2017 and August 23, 2025 may be eligible for a share of a $4.75 million national settlement. The underlying lawsuit, brought against Otsuka Canada Pharmaceutical and Lundbeck Canada, alleged the manufacturers didn't adequately warn patients or doctors that the drug could trigger or worsen compulsive behaviours — things like problem gambling, hypersexuality, binge eating, or compulsive shopping.

To qualify, you need to have taken Rexulti in that window and be able to point to one or more of those compulsive-behaviour side effects. Compensation is split into two tracks — psychological harm and financial loss from the behaviour — and claimants can potentially recover under both. Payouts range widely: reported figures run from roughly $6,500 for milder cases up toward $102,000 for the most severe, documented ones.

Filing isn't a one-line form. You'll need supporting documentation — pharmacy or medical records showing you were prescribed and took the drug, plus evidence of the specific harm (gambling records, spending records, employment records, or similar, depending on your claim type).
